Book of the Dead
Wondrous Item

Requires Attunement: Cleric or Warlock

This ancient tome, bound in dark leather and inscribed with faintly glowing runes, serves as both a conduit to the dead and a protector of the living. Its yellowed pages seem endless, filling with names as the wielder's connection to life and death deepens.

Abilities
Communion with the Departed

You always have the spell Speak with Dead prepared, and it does not count against the spells you know.

You can cast Speak with Dead once per long rest without expending a spell slot or requiring material components by writing the name of the deceased into the book.

Names of the Bound

The book can hold a number of names equal to your proficiency bonus. With your permission, a creature can use its action to inscribe its name into the book.

Twilight Ward

When a creature whose name is in the book would be reduced to 0 hit points (but not killed outright), you can use your reaction to summon a ward that absorbs the damage.

The damage is reflected back to the attacker, who must make a Constitution saving throw against your spell save DC:

  • Failure: The attacker takes half the damage inflicted on the target.
  • Success: The attacker takes no damage, and the ward dissipates.

Once triggered, no creature can benefit from this ability until you finish a long rest.

Sending Across the Veil

You can cast Sending targeting a creature whose name is in the book without expending a spell slot or requiring material components. To do so, write your message in the book.

The target hears the message in their mind. If they reply, their message appears on the page instead of in your mind. The writing disappears after 1 minute.

Erasure

As an action, you can magically erase any name from the book by touching it.

“A keeper of names, a guide to the lost, and a shield to those in peril—this tome bridges the gap between the worlds of the living and the dead.”
